\# DIP Governance Maturity Model



This document describes how governance of the Deterministic Integrity Protocol (DIP) evolves over time.



DIP governance is intentionally structured to progress through defined maturity phases.



The goal is to protect protocol integrity during early development while enabling long-term institutional governance.



---



\# Governance Phases



\## Phase 1 — Foundational Stewardship



Characteristics:



\- Protocol specification is defined

\- Invariants are established

\- Canonicalization rules are frozen

\- Conformance policy is defined

\- Reference implementation exists



Governance structure:



\- Stewardship authority protects invariants

\- Amendment procedure exists but is rarely used

\- Governance decisions are tightly controlled to prevent early drift



Primary goal:



Protect protocol integrity during formation.



---



\## Phase 2 — Institutional Adoption



Trigger conditions:



\- Multiple independent implementations exist

\- Institutional users begin integration

\- Conformance ecosystem expands



Governance evolution:



\- Steward authority begins narrowing

\- Security review processes expand

\- External contributors participate in protocol discussion



Primary goal:



Ensure protocol stability across ecosystem growth.



---



\## Phase 3 — Multi-Stakeholder Governance



Trigger conditions:



\- Protocol is widely implemented

\- Independent institutions depend on DIP verification

\- Multiple maintainers exist



Governance structure evolves toward:



\- Governance council

\- Formal amendment voting

\- Multi-party review requirements



Primary goal:



Distribute authority while preserving invariants.



---



\## Phase 4 — Institutional Infrastructure



Long-term state.



Characteristics:



\- DIP functions as neutral infrastructure

\- Protocol evolution becomes rare

\- Backward verification remains guaranteed indefinitely



Governance characteristics:



\- Constitutional rules dominate

\- Amendments require extremely high thresholds

\- Institutional review replaces individual authority



Primary goal:



Ensure long-term protocol durability.



---



\# Governance Safeguards



Across all phases:



The following principles remain invariant:



\- Canonical determinism must never change silently

\- Historical artifacts must remain verifiable

\- Cryptographic binding must remain explicit

\- Protocol invariants must remain enforceable



Governance evolution cannot violate protocol invariants.



---



\# Founder Stewardship Context



During early phases, stewardship exists to protect protocol integrity.



Stewardship authority narrows over time as governance matures.



This structure ensures:



\- Early protection

\- Gradual decentralization

\- Long-term institutional stability



---



\# Final Principle



DIP governance prioritizes durability over speed.



Protocols intended to operate across decades must evolve slowly, transparently, and with strong safeguards.



This maturity model exists to ensure that outcome.
